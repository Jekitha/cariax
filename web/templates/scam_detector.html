Common Career Scams to Watch For

Upfront Fee Scams

Legitimate employers never ask candidates to pay for training, equipment, or job placement. Any request for money before starting work is a major red flag.

Work From Home Scams

Be wary of "easy money" work-from-home jobs promising high earnings for simple tasks like data entry, surveys, or package reshipping.

Identity Theft Schemes

Scammers may pose as employers to collect personal information like Aadhaar, PAN, or bank details for identity theft.

Pyramid/MLM Schemes

If the "job" focuses on recruiting others or requires buying products to sell, it's likely a pyramid scheme disguised as employment.

How to Protect Yourself

  • • Research the company thoroughly before applying
  • • Never pay money to get a job
  • • Don't share sensitive personal information until you've verified the employer
  • • Be suspicious of unsolicited job offers
  • • Trust your instincts - if it seems too good to be true, it probably is
